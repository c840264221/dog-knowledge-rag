## 📖 关于该犬种
The Romanian Carpathian Shepherd dog is a natural guardian, very courageous and loyal, distinguishing itself by its unconditional attachment and devotion to the whole family, or the herd it protects and to its master. The Carpathian is a dignified, proud, calm, and balanced dog.The Romanian Carpathian Shepherds are fierce guardians, brave, intelligent, loyal, and proud dogs that are also very playful, and fully devoted to their family and master - they will listen to all family, but recognize one human to be the ultimate master they will follow. The Carpathians are large, double coated dogs, gray wolf-like in color, very agile and powerful. They are extremely efficient working dogs, naturally selected to guard and protect herds and flocks from the large numbers of gray wolves, brown bears and other predators housed in the Carpathian Mountains of Romania, the dogs playing an important role in maintain a balanced, natural habitat between humans, domesticated animals, and wild predators in the area.Most Carpathian Shepherds today are still working in the Romanian mountains, performing the same jobs they were selected for hundreds of years ago, and, when they are not doing that, they are loyal companions and vigilant guardians of homes and families. Frequently described as ¿noble¿ dogs due to their proud and dignified elegance, the Carpathians are big and strong, standing as high as 30 inches at the shoulder and often weighing more than 100 pounds. These fierce guardians usually are very calm and balanced, appearing indifferent at times, while preserving their energy for the real danger. At the right moment, they will quickly spring into action and move with unexpected agility, speed and determination to meet a threat.Club: Romanian Carpathian Shepherd Club of AmericaWebsite: www.carpathianshepherd.orgPronunciation - Romanian Car-pay-thee-uhn Shepherd

## 🧾 历史
The Romanian Carpathian Shepherd Dog has been used for centuries by the Romanian human shepherds in the Carpathian Mountains to defend the herds and guard the family or tourist cabins, houses, and barns - the Carpathian Mountains of Romania being known to this day for housing more than 50% of Europes brown bear population and more than 37% of Europes gray wolves.The selection of the Romanian Carpathian Shepherd breed was performed naturally from an endemic dog breed present in the Carpathian-Danubio area. The selection was done initially based on performance and efficiency in guarding and protection and the first breed standard was written in 1934 by the Zootechnical National Institute of Romania. The Carpathian Shepherds¿ international and definitive recognition came in 2015 when hundreds of dogs with the same characteristics and temperament were presented in front of international canine judges and the breed standard as we know it today, was written and published by the International Canine Federation as FCI Standard 350.

## 🩺 养护指南
### Health
The Romanian Carpathian Shepherds are a naturally selected breed in the country of origin, most breeders still letting mother nature play an important role in each pup's survival. For this reason, the purebred Romanian Carpathian Shepherds have no known genetical issues.  They naturally eat much less than expected for their size and they will usually stop eating when they are full. However, insisting on overfeeding them can become a problem if not controlled.

### Grooming
The Romanian Carpathians Shepherds¿ furry double-coat is dense to protect them equally from cold and heat and it varies from short on the front of the limbs and head to long on the tail, back of limbs, chest, and around the neck. They shed a lot of the undercoat when the warm seasons are starting, around May-June. Brushing is necessary occasionally, using a de-shedding brush, or a dog undercoat rake. For the areas with longer fur, a de-matting brush works best. Nails can be trimmed, teeth brushed, and ears cleaned regularly. ### Exercise
The Romanian Carpathian Shepherd dogs walk along with their flock up on the mountains, keeping guard. They usually preserve their energy to be able to protect the herd from a bear, boar, wolf, or bobcat attack, which happen often and are expected in the Carpathian Mountains. If they don¿t have a herd to guard, they need at least 1-2 hours a day moderate activity: walks, nose work, puzzles, and play time. They are mostly pack motivated much more than food and toy motivated. It¿s necessary to have a fenced yard, or be kept on a leash, as they do like to roam on their territory.

### Training
The Romanian Carpathian Shepherds were bred to have a mind of their own, and to work with little human guidance, watching and protecting their flock first and foremost. Highly intelligent and with strong personalities, the Carpathians respond very well to camaraderie games played with humans and with each other and learn very fast by first observing and then applying. They respond naturally well to standard obedience training, however most of them are usually not motivated by food or toys in the long run. If the dogs are not working guarding herds or farm animals, they need nose work, puzzles, and other fun games to keep their minds and bodies stimulated.

### Nutrition
In their natural habitat, the adult Romanian Carpathian Shepherd dogs eat polenta or bread soaked in milk (sheep, goat, cow) or in the very nutritious water left from cheese making. They eat once or twice daily, even though they can sustain longer periods without food, which sometimes happens up on the mountains. They occasionally eat raw and/or cooked meat and cooked chicken, rice, and vegetables stews. Puppies eat kibble soaked in milk, usually 3 times a day. A vetenarian recommended, balanced quality diet of low-protein large breed formula may be a good choice for them, however they will always prefer wet food instead.
