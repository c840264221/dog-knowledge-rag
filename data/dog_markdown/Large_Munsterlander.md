## 📖 关于该犬种
The pleasing nature of the Large Munsterlander provides a cooperative and reliable companion in the field and home.In accordance with its hunting purpose as a versatile gun dog, the Large Munsterlander must have all the facilities required of it for all work in the field, forest, and water both before and after the shot. This noble and tractable hunter applies an intelligent nose backed by a determined will to search, point, track, and retrieve upland birds, waterfowl, and furred game. Their desire to please you extends into the home where the Large Munsterlander is a calm, loving, and loyal family member.

## 🧾 历史
The historical development of the Large Munsterlander goes back to the white/parti-colour bird and hawking dog of the Middle Ages via the Stöberhund and Wachtelhund to the Pointer of the 19th century. The Large Munsterlander as well as the Small Munsterlander and the German Longhaired Pointer belong to the family of the longhaired German Pointing Dogs, whose planned breeding began towards the end of the 19th Century. After the German Longhair Club finally excluded the black color from breeding in 1909, the  Club for the pure breeding of the black and white Münsterland Pointers (founded in 1919) took over the breeding of the black and white Longhair. After incorporating the remainder of these native Longhairs, in particular from West-Münsterland and lower Saxony, onto a foundation list, the Club began the planned breeding of the Large Munsterlander in 1922.Responsive and versatile, the Large Munsterlander arose from the need for an all inclusive gun dog who would hunt, point, track, and retrieve upland birds, waterfowl, and game in fields, forests, and water. Passionate retrievers in and out of the water, Large Munsterlanders excel at finding their quarry before and after the gunshot.

## 🩺 养护指南
### Health
Large Munsterlanders are a robust breed. Working with a responsible breeder, those wishing to own a Large Munsterlander can gain the education they need to know about specific health concerns within the breed. Good breeders utilize genetic testing of their breeding stock to reduce the likelihood of disease in their puppies.

### Grooming
Occasional brushings will maintain the quality of the coat with the occasional bath to keep them clean and looking their best. Large Munsterlanders are seasonal shedders and as such it is important to maintain their coats more frequently in the late spring and early fall. Their nails should be trimmed regularly with a nail clipper or grinder to avoid overgrowth, splitting, and cracking. Their ears should be checked regularly to avoid a buildup of wax and debris, which can result in infection. Teeth should be brushed regularly.

### Exercise
Large Munsterlanders make wonderful family companions, however they do require frequent exercise and stimulation. The breed was born to hunt and that is the task they are happiest at. This is an all-purpose, versatile hunting breed that was developed to hunt, point, retrieve, and track game.     The breed's hunting heritage means they have a high activity level and need appropriate outlets for their energy, including daily exercise. If you don't hunt with your Large Munsterlander, be sure to give them other ways to keep active, such as going for a run or playing in a fenced area.  If proper stimulation and exercise are not provided, they will wander and self-hunt when left to their own devices.

### Training
Large Munsteranders are eager to please, intelligent and very trainable. They possess a high energy and, therefore, need and deserve lots of attention and exercise.

### Nutrition
Large Munsterlanders have the same kind of nutritional needs as all other sporting breed.  Large Munsterlanders are a medium-large breed and may have a lifespan ranging from 12 to 14 years.     What you feed your dog is an individual choice, but working with your veterinarian and/or breeder will be the best way to determine frequency of meals as a puppy and the best adult diet to increase his longevity. Clean, fresh water should be available at all times.
