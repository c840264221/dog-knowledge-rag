## 📖 关于该犬种
The Pont-Audemer Spaniel is used to hunt a variety of fowl.  They are a medium size dog. It's a rustic and courageous dog, a faithful companion who is intelligent and unique.Because of its physique and its relative rarity, the Pont-Audemer Spaniel undoubtedly holds the prize for originality. With his curly crest covering the top of his head and his long ears, well trimmed with wavy silk, he seems to be wearing a "great century" wig, perfectly framing his head. Its dense fur allows it to resist water in the coldest temperatures and to face the thickest thickets and brush.The Pont-Audemer is above all a hunting dog. They are hardy and hard working, but have the Spaniel traits of being gentle, affectionate and easy to train. Originally developed to hunt water fowl in the marsh they are water specialists but also experts at pointing and flushing putting them in the Pointing Dog groups in other registries. They have respectable tracking skills, are strong in their field search, water work and retrieving abilities.Pontos are medium sized and adapt very well to urban life if they are allowed long regular outings to let off steam for their physical and mental balance. Their instinctual cautiousness should not be mistaken for timidness.They thrive on lots of love and sincerity from their family. They have blind confidence in us and its up to us not to disappoint them. They are thrilled to be up before dawn to head out for a good hunt but just as happy to get home at the end of their day to cuddle with their family.They are a rustic and courageous dog, a faithful and cheerful companion!

## 🧾 历史
They were developed in the 19th century in the area of Pont-Audemer France using Setters and Pointers brought over by English sportsmen. However the the unique appearance and hunting style is attributed to the English and Irish Water Spaniels that were included in their creation. The marshes in the area surrounding Pont-Audemer provided them the opportunity to hunt water fowl and then head to the field for upland game.  Unfortunately in the 1880¿s they population declined for a variety of reasons and struggled in number.  In 1881 the French cynologist, James De Connick, dedicated himself to increasing the quality and quantity of Pontos thru selective breeding and crossing to ancestral breeds including the Irish Water Spaniel.  However, as with many French breeds, the last two World Wars pushed them to near extinction and by 1980 there were not enough pups being whelped for the Pont-Audemer club to maintain its status with the French kennel club.  Soon after the breed was included in the Picardy Spaniel club in Europe where it remains today.

## 🩺 养护指南
### Grooming
Their coat does best with weekly brushing, checking for mats in the ears.  The coat and skin stays healthiest when clean but the curls will keep their crispness if not over shampooed.  Keep nails trimmed weekly.  Check and clean ears weekly and after field work.

### Exercise
Daily exercise is optimum but it can come from a variety of activities. They are quick learners and love to interact with their family members. But they are happy cuddling with their family.  A fully fenced yard will keep them safe from following their sharp nose after an interesting scent.

### Training
Pont-Audemer Spaniels and intelligent with sharp instincts. They need a handler who understands and respects that. They are natural bird dogs who generally only need exposure to game to develop their skills. They do best when exposed to birds and shot at an early age. They are sensible while cautious.Quick and agile, they are light on their feet.  There are many arenas of dog sports where this breed would excel.

### Nutrition
Well-balanced died suited to the age and activity of the dog.
